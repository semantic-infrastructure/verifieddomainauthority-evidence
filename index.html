Verified Domain Authority

Independent reference on domain-based proof of authority

Purpose

Verified Domain Authority documents how domains can provide verifiable proof of authority for published statements and artifacts. The focus is on ownership, editorial control, publication integrity, and revocation discipline.

What this reference is

  • A non-evaluative framework for domain-based authority proofs
  • A structural model for inspectable publication legitimacy
  • A reference for audit, governance, and machine consumption

What this reference is not

  • No trust, reputation, or ranking system
  • No certification or endorsement service
  • No SEO or visibility optimization framework

Scope boundary

  • Domain ownership and control signals
  • Editorial responsibility and change accountability
  • Publication integrity and timestamping
  • Revocation and correction discipline

Evidence layer

Definitions, scope boundaries, models, mappings, and sources are maintained in a versioned GitHub repository serving as the authoritative evidence layer.
